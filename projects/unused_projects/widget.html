    Credit score meets online banking

    "I think I'm excited about it (product/widget). I think that's a great thing for people to monitor their own credit this way." - User

     

    Background

    Since SavvyMoney is a B2B product offering, the point of entry into the product is through online banking (OLB). Users log into their OLB, go to the "credit score widget" to sign-up a use the service.

     


    Challenges

    1. Lack of Consistency in widget components across different platforms due to variety in sizes.
    2. Low product engagement due to the high visibility of score made it unintuitive for the users to click over to the full product and leverage credit monitoring and personalized recommendations.
    3. Non-responsiveness of the widgets caused friction in the experience by not responding to the OLB iframe widths.


     

    Interactive Widgets

    The goal of the new designs was to increase engagement and provide relevant information the users, at a relevant time. 

    "I'm sure with (old design) i would get (to different pages) eventually but there's not a quick link on it, (new design) quick links is very helpful. I would have to look harder to find information in (old design)." - User

    The new widgets had 4 major components:

    1. Score and Rating: This is the most recently pulled score by the user and subsequent rating. A "refresh score" feature was added such that user can refresh their score while in the OLB.
    2. Navigation: This let the user to directly navigate to they information they are looking for, directly from the widget.
    3. Badge Count: This reflected the alerts of the users: change in score, monitoring alerts etc. This was an attempt to draw attention of the user to the most relevant information at the time.
    4. Offers: This area presented the offer recommendations that the user may be eligible for. The layout of this section was cleaned to highlight the value- proposition of the offer.

    The designs were tested with users before implementation. There was positive acceptance of the new widget designs and the widget tested well against the criteria of usability and satisfaction.


    "You're on your bank account and then all of a sudden your peripheral vision you see a little red alert you're going to click and look at it more than if it was just sitting there."- User

    Impact

    1. The widgets had an immediate and huge impact on engagement. The clicks were increase by 30 times and the % increase varied from 500–3000%.
    2. Another noticeable impact was that the offer section (on of the common factors between old and new designs) saw an increased engagement and more users were clicking over them.
    3. The concept of badge count formed the basis of the mobile strategy while rolling out the SavvyMoney solution to the mobile banking platforms.
